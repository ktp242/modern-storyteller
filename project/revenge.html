Revenge
Yu-Fei (Eva) Kao’s Revenge (2014) is a 2D cel-animation based on a poem by Joseph Silver that explores a revenge cycle within a couple’s life. It is a strange story about betrayal that could happen to anyone and has a crazy unexpected ending. To produce the dream-like world presented in Revenge, Kao created separately painted gouache on paper layers, which were then scanned and digitally altered under superimposed fragments of hand-lettered words. Revenge’s poetic depiction of infidelity is a tender space for recovery from the emotional torment associated with betrayal.

Yu Fei Kao 高郁斐


Yu-Fei Kao (Eva) is an award-winning Taiwanese motion graphic designer and 2D animator currently living and working in New York City, who specializes in 2D cel-animation, stop motion, visual effects, and compositing. Kao received a BA in graphic design from Tamkang University in Taiwan in 2011 and her MFA in Computer Art in 2014 with a concentration in motion graphics from the School of Visual Arts in New York City. Beginning in 2011, she worked as a motion graphic designer for the Taipei-based design studio Silly Creative, where she focused on visual image design for music concerts. Her projects include motion graphic designs for international concerts by A-mei, Tanya, Jolin Tsai and the MTV Awards in Asia. Currently, she is assisting with motion graphics for Deep Focus in New York. Kao’s merger of a handmade aesthetic with inventive typography seamlessly achieves fun and inspirational designs that skillfully deliver key concepts.
